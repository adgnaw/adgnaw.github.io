About me

I am curently working on a variety of predictive modeling projects at LLN Technologies.

I was a quantitative researcher at Aigen Investment Management. Before that I was with Two Sigma Investments, joining the firm after obtaining my Ph.D. in 2014.

I did my graduate studies in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS) at MIT from 2008 to 2014. I was a member of Professor Gregory Wornell's Signals, Information and Algorithms laboratory in the Research Laboratory of Electronics (RLE). You can read more about my research here. I was featured in the April 2013 RLE Student Spotlight.

I obtained my Bachelor of Applied Sciences (major in electrical engineering + minor in mathematics) from the Electrical and Computer Engineering department at the University of Toronto.
